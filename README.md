## **Project Report: Assessing Regional Conflict Dynamics in Nigeria**

# **Executive Summary** 
This analysis provides a data-driven overview of security incidents across various Nigerian states in 2025. By auditing and standardizing 17 unique conflict reports, this project identifies critical "hotspots" and analyzes the fatality of different incident types. The primary goal is to provide actionable intelligence for resource allocation and strategic security planning.

# **Fatalities by State; (Identifying Regional Hotspots):**
The data reveals a significant geographic concentration of high-impact security events in the Northern region.
<img width="628" height="247" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/7c877e84-0ab4-4be8-9586-eb0b073a88b6" />

Borno State recorded the highest number of fatalities, totaling 17 deaths across three major incidents. This confirms the state’s status as a primary zone for high-intensity armed conflict.

Kano State followed as the second most impacted area with 16 fatalities.

In contrast, states like Bauchi, Edo, Enugu, Kaduna, Rivers, and Sokoto recorded 0 fatalities within the reported period, indicating either a period of relative stability or lower-intensity incident types (such as non-violent theft).

# **Analytical Summary**
The data demonstrates a clear correlation between Incident Type and Geography. The North-East (Borno) remains the epicenter for high-fatality Armed Conflict, whereas regions like the FCT (Abuja) face a higher prevalence of Kidnapping and Theft with lower fatality rates.

A critical observation is that while Theft is widespread, its contribution to the overall death toll is minimal (13%), suggesting that the current security threat to human life is primarily driven by organized armed groups and kidnapping syndicates rather than petty crime.

# **Conclusive Recommendations**
Based on these findings, the following strategic actions are recommended:

**Borno/Kano Deployment:** Security assets should prioritize heavy-infantry and counter-insurgency capabilities in Borno and Kano to address the 57% lethality rate driven by Armed Conflict.

**Intelligence-Led Policing in FCT:** In FCT and surrounding areas, resources should shift toward intelligence-gathering and rapid response units focused on kidnapping prevention.

**Standardized Reporting:** The inconsistencies found in the raw data (e.g., mislabeling FCT as Rivers) highlight the need for a unified digital reporting system to prevent intelligence gaps.
